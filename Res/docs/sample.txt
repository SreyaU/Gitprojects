Here is a **complete breakdown of Wi-Fi standards (IEEE 802.11)** — structured for deep technical understanding, validation, and interview readiness.

## 📌 1. **Wi-Fi Standards Overview**

| **Wi-Fi Gen** | **IEEE Standard** | **Release Year** | **Band(s)**     | **Max PHY Rate** | **Modulation** | **Channel Width** | **Key Features**                 |
| ------------- | ----------------- | ---------------- | --------------- | ---------------- | -------------- | ----------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Wi-Fi 1       | 802.11b           | 1999             | 2.4 GHz         | 11 Mbps          | DSSS/CCK       | 20 MHz            | Legacy; low data rates           |
| Wi-Fi 2       | 802.11a           | 1999             | 5 GHz           | 54 Mbps          | OFDM           | 20 MHz            | OFDM in 5 GHz                    |
| Wi-Fi 3       | 802.11g           | 2003             | 2.4 GHz         | 54 Mbps          | OFDM + DSSS    | 20 MHz            | Backward-compatible with 802.11b |
| Wi-Fi 4       | 802.11n           | 2009             | 2.4 + 5 GHz     | 600 Mbps         | 64-QAM         | 20/40 MHz         | MIMO, Aggregation, SGI           |
| Wi-Fi 5       | 802.11ac          | 2013             | **5 GHz only**  | 6.9 Gbps         | 256-QAM        | 20–160 MHz        | MU-MIMO (DL), Beamforming        |
| Wi-Fi 6       | 802.11ax          | 2019             | 2.4 + 5 GHz     | 9.6 Gbps         | 1024-QAM       | 20–160 MHz        | OFDMA, TWT, MU-MIMO UL/DL        |
| Wi-Fi 6E      | 802.11ax (amend.) | 2020             | 6 GHz           | 9.6 Gbps         | 1024-QAM       | 20–160 MHz        | 6 GHz clean band, more channels  |
| Wi-Fi 7       | 802.11be          | \~2024–25        | 2.4 + 5 + 6 GHz | 46 Gbps          | 4096-QAM       | 20–320 MHz        | MLO, 16x16 MIMO, puncturing      |
